We will use units 
used in high energy
physics in which $\hbar$ (Plank's constant divided by $2\pi$) and $c$ (speed of light) are set to 1 
temporarily, and then restored when one needs actual values in MKS units. in such units,
everything has dimensions of $L^y$, where $L$
stands for length, and $y$ is some rational number. The dimensions of length and time are $L$ and those of energy, mass and
momentum are $L^{-1}$

Let

$x=$ length scale,
pixel density, number of pixels per inch (or any unit of length)

$a=$ lattice spacing in inches (or any unit of length),
distance between two adjacent pixels, resolution length

$b\in[1, \infty]$, magnification

$\mu=$ renormalization mass scale, in units
of mass or energy

$\Lam=$ momentum cutoff, in units of momentum

Consider\footnote{It is common to
use a prime for the output
instead of a 0 subscript 
for the input. For example, to say
$a'=ba$ instead of $a=ba_0$. But I think this
form makes the functional
dependence of all quantities more clear.
}
\beq
a(b)= ba_0
\eeq\beq
 x(b)= \frac{x_0}{b}
\eeq\beq
 \mu(b)=\frac{\mu_0}{b}
\eeq\beq
 \Lam(b) = \frac{\Lam_0}{b}
\eeq

\beq
\ln \mu 
=
\ln \mu_0 -\ln b
\implies \quad d\ln \mu = - d\ln b
\eeq

High energy physics uses $\mu$ 
and momentum space renormalization
and integrating out high frequency modes.
Condensed Matter uses real space renormalization and coarse graining.

near a critical point,
\beq
t(b)=b^{y_t}t_0
\label{eq-t-scales-as}
\eeq
consider the 2-dim Ising model 
and let $t = \frac{T-T_c}{T_c}$.
Near the  critical point,
Eq.(\ref{eq-t-scales-as})
is satisfied with $y_t=1$.
So temperature deviation grows linearly under coarse-graining.
Hence, the temperature is a relevant operator near the critical pointpoint.

\beq
\ln t =y_t \ln b + \ln t_0
\implies
\frac{d\ln t}{d\ln b} = y_t,\quad-\;\frac{1}{t}\frac{dt}{d\ln \mu}=y_t
\eeq

\section{Fractal Dimension}
Let

$N=$  number of branches
that each branch splits into, number of pieces that each piece breaks into.

$b=$ length (not area or volume) of initial piece
divided by length of final piece

$D=$ fractal dimension, $D>0$ can be less than or greater than one.

\beq
b^D = N, \quad D = \frac{\ln N}{\ln b}
\eeq

examples:
\begin{itemize}
\item line segment

\item square

\item Cantor set

\end{itemize}
