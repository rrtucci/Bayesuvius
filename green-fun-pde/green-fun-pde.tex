\section{Introduction}
Suppose

\beq
L_{x,t} \eta(x,t) = S(x,t)
\eeq
where

$L_{x, t}=\partial_t -\partial_x^2\quad$ for {\bf Diffusion Equation (DE)}


$L_{x, t}=\partial_t^2 -\partial_x^2\quad$ for {\bf Wave Equation (WE)}

$L_{x, t}=\quad -\partial_x^2\quad$   for {\bf 1-dim Laplace Equation}\footnote{Many workers call $\nabla^2 \eta=0$ Laplace's equation and $\nabla^2 \eta=S$ Poisson's Equation. I will
refer to both as Laplace's Equation.}






The Diffusion Equation (DE) in one space dimension $x$,
 with a source $S(x,t)$, is

In this chapter will illustrate the use of Green
functions by solving the DE partial
differential equation (PDE)
for all $x>0$ and $t>0$, with  
given initial
conditions at $t=0$
and given boundary conditions (BC) of 3 possible types
at $x=0$.

\section{Boundary Condition Types}
Types of boundary conditions (BC) that we will consider:

\begin{itemize}
\item {\bf Dirichlet BC} (sticky, inverted reflection wall)

\beq \eta(0, t)= D_\rvt(t)
\eeq

\item
{\bf Neumann BC} (slippery, reflecting wall)


\beq
\partial_x\eta(0,t)= N_\rvt(t)
\eeq
For example, if there were an impermeable wall at $x=0$,

\beq
\partial_x\eta(0,t)=0
\eeq

$L\eta=S$ and Neumann BC
only constrain derivatives of $\eta$.
Hence, $\eta+c$ and
$\eta$ for some constant $c$, both 
satisfy those constraints; i.e., $\eta$ is 
defined only  up to a constant.

\item {\bf Mixed (Robin) BC}

\beq
[a_0 \eta + a_1\partial_x\eta  ](0, t)=R_\rvt(t)
\eeq
\end{itemize}
If the boundary condition depends on $\eta(x,t)$
only (i.e., if $D_\rvt=N_\rvt=R_\rvt=0$), then it is called a {\bf homogeneous BC}.
Otherwise, it is said to be an {\bf inhomogeneous BC}.

\section{Initial Condition Types}
Initial Conditions (IC)

\begin{itemize}
\item {\bf No IC} for {\bf Elliptic PDEs}  like Laplace Equation  $\partial_x^2\eta
+\partial_y^2\eta=0$
 that have no time derivatives.

\item {\bf First order IC} for {\bf Hyperbolic PDEs}  like Diffusion Equation  $\partial_t\eta=D\partial_x^2\eta$
 that are first order
in time derivative
\beq \eta(x,0)= \eta_\rvx(x)
\eeq

\item {\bf Cauchy IC} for {\bf Parabolic PDEs} like Wave Equation  $\partial^2_t\eta=c^2\partial_x^2\eta$ that are second order in time derivative 

\beq \eta(x,0)= \eta_\rvx(x),\quad
\partial_t\eta(x,0) = C_\rvx(x)
\eeq

\end{itemize}

\subsection{Notation and Conventions},
I will often use specialized 
notation in this chapter to
express with more clarity 
the basic Green's functions equations and their
derivations. This section
will describe such notation.

An event
 will be presented 
either as a row vector or a column vector:
\beq
(x,t)\leftrightarrow \oneevent{x}{t}
\eeq
Functions $f()$ of one event, and functions $G()$
of two events
will be expressed with their events either as row or column vectors
\beq
f(x,t) \leftrightarrow f\oneevent{x}{t}
\eeq
\beq
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}\leftrightarrow G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq

Another notation that we will often use for 
expressing functions of one or two
events, is Dirac bra-ket notation.
We describe that notation next.

Define
\beq
L_{x,t}= \partial_t -D \partial_x^2,\quad
L_{x,t} G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=\stackt{\delta(x-x')}{\delta(t-t')}
\eeq


\beq
L^\dagger_{x,t}= -{\partial}_t -D {\partial}_x^2,
\quad L^\dagger_{x,t}G^\dagger\twoevent{x'}{t'}{x}{t}=\stackt{\delta(x-x')}{\delta(t+t')}
\eeq

\beq
\engdim{D}\engdim{\partial_x^2} = 
\engdim{\partial_t}\implies 
\engdim{D} = L^2/T
\eeq

\beq
\engdim{\partial_t}\engdim{G}=
\engdim{\delta(x)}\engdim{\delta(t)}
\implies 
\engdim{G}=1/L
\eeq


\beq
P_\rvx = \int_0^\infty dx'\; \ket{\rvx=x'}\bra{\rvx=x'}
\eeq


\beq
P_\rvt = \int_0^t dt'\; \ket{\rvt=t'}\bra{\rvt=t'}
\eeq

\beq
P_0 = \ket{0}\bra{0}
\eeq

\beq
\partial_{\rvx} =
\int_0^\infty dx'\; \ket{\rvx=x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{\rvx=x'}
\eeq

\beq
\partial_{\rvt} =
\int_0^\infty dt\; \ket{\rvt=t'}\partial_{t'}\bra{\rvt=t'}
\eeq

\beq
\rv{L}= \partial_\rvt-D\partial^2_\rvx
\eeq

\beq
f(x)\cev{\partial_x} a(x)= a(x) \partial_x f(x)
\eeq
We will also use the following suggestive notation
for dual tensor product spaces



\beq \stackt{A}{B}= A\otimes B\eeq

\beq \kett{x}{t}=\ket{x}\otimes\ket{t},
\quad 
\brat{x}{t}=\bra{x}\otimes\bra{t}
\eeq

used in High Energy
Physics in which $\hbar$ (Plank's constant $h$ divided by $2\pi$) and $c$ (speed of light) are set to 1

everything has dimensions of $L^D$, where $L$
stands for length, and $D$ is some rational number. The dimensions of length and time are $L$ and those of energy, mass and
momentum are $L^{-1}$.

are dimensionless.

\subsection{General Solution}
\begin{claim}
Suppose
\beq
L_{x,t}= \partial_t -D \partial_x^2,\quad
L_{x,t} G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=\stackt{\delta(x-x')}{\delta(t-t')}
\eeq
The {\bf General Solution} to the equation
\beq
L_{x,t} \eta (x,t) = S(x,t)\quad
\eeq
for $x>0$ and $t>0$
with initial conditions
\beq
\eta(x,0)= \eta_\rvx(x)
\eeq
and satisfying Dirichlet, Neumann or Robin boundary conditions at $x=0$,
is:

\beqa
\ket{\eta}
&=&
\underbrace{G\stackt{P_\rvx}{P_\rvt}\ket{S}}_{\text{Source Term $\cala_S$}}
\\
&&+\underbrace{G\stackt{P_\rvx}{P_0}\kett{\eta_\rvx}{0}}_{\text
{Initial Conditions (IC) term  $\cala_{IC}$}}
\\
&&+\underbrace{D
G\stackt{\left[-\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}
+
\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right]_{x'=0}}{P_\rvt}
\ket{\eta}}_{\text{Boundary Conditions (BC) term $\cala_{BC}$}}
\eeqa
or, in non-Dirac notation,

\beqa
\eta\oneevent{x}{t}
&=&
\int_0^tdt'
\int_0^\infty dx'\;G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}S\oneevent{x'}{t'}
\\
&&+\int_0^\infty dx'\;
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{0}\eta_\rvx(x')
\\
&&+D
\int_0^tdt'\;G\twoevent{x}{t}{0}{t'} \left[-\partial_{x'}
+ \cev{\partial}_{x'}
\right]\eta\oneevent{0}{t'}
\eeqa
\end{claim}
\proof

Define $I$, $I_\rvx$ and $I_\rvt$  by
\beq
I = \brat{x}{t}G\rv{L}
\ket{\eta}-
\brat{x}{t}\rv{L^\dagger}G^\dagger
\ket{\eta}
\eeq

\beq
I_\rvt =
\brat{x}{t}G
\partial_\rvt
\ket{\eta}-
\brat{x}{t}
(-{\partial}_\rvt)G^\dagger
\ket{\eta}
\eeq

\beq
I_\rvx =
\brat{x}{t}G\partial_\rvx^2
\ket{\eta}-
\brat{x}{t}
\partial_\rvx^2G^\dagger
\ket{\eta}
\eeq
Hence
\beq
\boxed
{I = I_\rvt -D I_\rvx}
\eeq
\hrule
Note that we can simplify the
expression for $I$ as follows:



\beq
\rv{L}\ket{\eta}=\ket{S},
\quad
\rv{L^\dagger}G^\dagger=1
\eeq

\beq
\boxed
{I = \brat{x}{t}G\ket{S}-\brat{x}{t}\ket{\eta}
}
\eeq
\hrule
Note that we can simplify the
expression for $I_\rvt$ as follows:


\beqa
I_\rvt &=&
\int_0^t dt'
\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{1}{\ket{t'}\partial_{t'}\bra{t'}}
\ket{\eta}
+
\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{1}{\ket{t'}\cev{\partial}_{t'}\bra{t'}}
\ket{\eta}
\eeqa
where we used

\beq
 \bra{t'}G^\dagger\ket{t}=\bra{t}G\ket{t'}
\implies
\partial_{t'} \bra{t'}G^\dagger\ket{t}
=
\bra{t}G\ket{t'}\cev{\partial}_{t'}
\eeq

\beq
\partial_{t'} (\ket{t'}\bra{t'})=
\ket{t'}\partial_{t'}\bra{t'}
+\ket{t'}\cev{\partial}_{t'}\bra{t'}
\eeq


\beqa
I_\rvt &=&
\int_0^t dt'
\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{1}{\partial_{t'} (\ket{t'}\bra{t'})}
\ket{\eta}
\eeqa



\beq
\int_0^t
dt'\;\partial_{t'} (\ket{t'}\bra{t'})
=
\ket{t}\bra{t}-\ket{0}\bra{0}
\eeq
\beq
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t}= 0 \quad \text{if $x\neq x'$} \text{(no instant propagation)}
\eeq
 so


\beq
\boxed{I_\rvt =
-\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{P_\rvx}{\ket{0}\bra{0}}
\ket{\eta}}
\eeq
\hrule
Note that we can simplify the
expression for $I_\rvx$ as follows:

\beq
I_\rvx =
\int_0^\infty dx'
\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}^2\bra{x'}}{1}
\ket{\eta}
-
\brat{x}{t}
G\stackt{\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}^2\bra{x'}}{1}
\ket{\eta}
\eeq

\beq
\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}^2\bra{x'}
-\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}^2\bra{x'}=
\partial_{x'}\left(\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}\right)
-\left(\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right)\cev{\partial}_{x'}
\eeq

\beq
I_\rvx=
\int_0^\infty dx'
\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{\partial_{x'}(\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'})
-\left(\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right)\cev{\partial}_{x'}}{1}
\ket{\eta}
\eeq

\begin{align}
\int_0^\infty dx'\left[
\partial_{x'}(\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'})
-\left(\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right)\cev{\partial}_{x'}\right]
&=
\left[\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}
-
\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right]_0^\infty
\\
&=
-\left[\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}
-
\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right]_{x'=0}
\end{align}

\beq
\boxed{I_\rvx=
\brat{x}{t}G
\stackt{\left[-\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}
+
\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right]_{x'=0}}{P_\rvt}
\ket{\eta}}
\eeq


\qed

The BC term 
of the General Solution 
must be specialized for
each of the 3 possible
types of BC. We show how to do  that below.

Careful: Note that
\beq
\partial_t G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=-\partial_{t'}G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq
and
\beq
\partial_x G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=-\partial_{x'}G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq

\begin{claim}(space-like propagation for $t=t'=0$)

\beq
G\twoevent{x}{0}{x'}{0}= \delta(x-x')
\eeq
As a consequence of this, the General Solution
satisfies the IC.
\end{claim}
\proof
\beq
\int_{0}^{t}dt'\;
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}[\underbrace{\cev{\partial}_{t}}_{-\cev{\partial}_{t'}} -D\underbrace{\cev{\partial}_{x}^2}_{\cev{\partial}_{x'}^2}]
=\int_{0}^{t}dt'\;
\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t')
\eeq
so integrating the time derivative term

\beq
-G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t}
+G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{0}
-D\int_0^t dt'\;
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}\cev{\partial}^2_{x'}=\delta(x-x')
\eeq
For  an infinitesimal $t=\eps$,
only one term survives 
on the left hand side. Hence
\beq
G\twoevent{x}{\eps}{x'}{0}=\delta(x-x')
\eeq



Consider the
the master solution and its IC term in particular:
\beq
\eta(x,t)= \cala_S(x,t) + \cala_{IC}(x,t) + \cala_{BC}(x,t)
\eeq

\beq
\cala_{IC}(x,t)=\int_0^\infty dx'\;
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{0}\eta_\rvx(x')
\eeq
When $t=0$,

\beqa
\cala_{IC}(x,0)&=&\int_0^\infty dx'\;
\underbrace{G\twoevent{x}{0}{x'}{0}}_{\delta(x-x')}
\eta_\rvx(x')
\\
&=& \eta_\rvx(x)
\eeqa
\qed

\begin{claim} (time-like propagation for $x=x'=0$)
\label{cl-time-like-prop-de}

\beq
D \partial_{x'}G\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'} 
=
-D\partial_x G\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'}=
 \delta(t-t')
\eeq 
As a consequence of this,
the General Solution satisfies the BC.
Furthermore, 
the Green's Function
must satisfy the homogeneous
version of the inhomogeneous BC
satisfied by $\eta(x,t)$.
\end{claim}
\proof



Note that

\beq
\int_{0}^{\infty}dx'\;
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}[\underbrace{\cev{\partial}_{t}}_{-\cev{\partial}_{t'}} -D\underbrace{\cev{\partial}_{x}^2}_{\cev{\partial}_{x'}^2}]
=\int_{0}^{\infty}dx'\;
\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t')
\eeq
so integrating the second derivative and setting $x=0$

\beq
-\int_{0}^{\infty}dx'\;G\twoevent{0}{t}{x'}{t'}\cev{\partial}_{t'}+
DG\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}=\delta(t-t')
\eeq
$G$ peaks when $x=x'$ so when $x=0$,
we need only integrate over $[0,\eps]$
for infinitesimal $\eps$.

\beq
\underbrace{-\int_{0}^{\eps}dx'\;G\twoevent{0}{t}{x'}{t'}\cev{\partial}_{t'}}_{0}
+
DG\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}=\delta(t-t')
\eeq


Consider the
the master solution and its BC term in particular:
\beq
\eta(x,t)= \cala_S(x,t) + \cala_{IC}(x,t) + \cala_{BC}(x,t)
\eeq
\beq
\cala_{BC}=
D
\int_0^tdt'\;G\twoevent{x}{t}{0}{t'} \left[-\partial_{x'}
+ \cev{\partial}_{x'}
\right]\eta\oneevent{0}{t'}
\eeq
The form of $\cala_{BC}$ and Claim
\ref{cl-time-like-prop-de} imply that
the Green's Function
must satisfy the homogeneous
version of the inhomogeneous BC
satisfied by $\eta(x,t)$.

Indeed, if Dirichlet BC
are satisfied

\beq
\eta(0,t)=D_\rvt(t),\quad G\twoevent{0}{t}{x'}{t'}=0
\eeq
then $\cala_{BC}(0, t)$ reduces to
\beqa
\cala_{BC}(0,t)&=&
\int_0^tdt'\;\underbrace{DG\twoevent{0} {t}{0}{t'}[\cev{\partial}_{x'}]}_{\delta(t-t')}
\eta\oneevent{0}{t'}
\\
&=&
D_\rvt(t)
\eeqa

Likewise, if Neumann BC
are satisfied

\beq
\partial_x\eta(0,t)=N_\rvt(t),\quad \partial_x G\twoevent{0}{t}{x'}{t'}=0
\eeq
then $\partial_x\cala_{BC}(0, t)$ reduces to
\beqa
\partial_x\cala_{BC}(0, t)&=&
\int_0^tdt'\;\underbrace{D\partial_x G\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'}}_{-\delta(t-t')} \left[-\partial_{x'}
\right]\eta\oneevent{0}{t'}
\\
&=&
N_\rvt(t)
\eeqa
\qed




\begin{mybluearea}
 
 For the Wave Equation, one must make the following modifications. 
\begin{claim}
Suppose
\beq
L_{x,t}= \partial_t^2 - \partial_x^2,\quad
L_{x,t} G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=\stackt{\delta(x-x')}{\delta(t-t')}
\eeq
The {\bf General Solution} to the equation
\beq
L_{x,t} \eta (x,t) = S(x,t)\quad
\eeq
for $x>0$ and $t>0$
with \beq
\eta(x,0)= \eta_\rvx(x),
\quad \partial_x\eta(x,0)=C_\rvx(x)
\eeq
and satisfying Dirichlet, Neumann or Robin boundary conditions at $x=0$,
is:



\beqa
\ket{\eta}
&=&
\underbrace{G\stackt{P_\rvx}{P_\rvt}\ket{S}}_{\text{Source Term $\cala_S$}}
\\
&&+\underbrace{G
\stackt{P_\rvx}{\left[\ket{t'}\partial_{t'}\bra{t'}
-
\ket{t'}\cev{\partial}_{t'}\bra{t'}\right]_{t'=0}}}_
{\text{Initial Conditions (IC) term  $\cala_{IC}$}}
\\
&&+\underbrace{
G\stackt{\left[-\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}
+
\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right]_{x'=0}}{P_\rvt}
\ket{\eta}}_{\text{Boundary Conditions (BC) term $\cala_{BC}$}}
\eeqa
or, in non-Dirac notation,

\beqa
\eta\oneevent{x}{t}
&=&
\int_0^tdt'
\int_0^\infty dx'\;G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}S\oneevent{x'}{t'}
\\
&&+\int_0^\infty dx'\;
G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{0}
\left[\partial_{t'}
- \cev{\partial}_{t'}
\right]\eta_\rvx\oneevent{x'}{0}
\\
&&+
\int_0^tdt'\;G\twoevent{x}{t}{0}{t'} \left[-\partial_{x'}
+ \cev{\partial}_{x'}
\right]\eta\oneevent{0}{t'}
\eeqa
\end{claim}
\proof
\qed

The BC term 
of the General Solution 
must be specialized for
each of the 3 possible
types of BC. We show how to do  that below.

Careful: Note that
\beq
\partial_t G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=-\partial_{t'}G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq
and
\beq
\partial_x G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=-\partial_{x'}G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq

\begin{claim}(space-like propagation for $t=t'=0$)

\beq
G\twoevent{x}{0}{x'}{0}=\delta(x-x')
\eeq

\beq
 -\partial_{t'}G\twoevent{x}{0}{x'}{0} 
=
\partial_t G\twoevent{x}{0}{x'}{0}=
 \delta(x-x')
\eeq 
As a consequence of this, the General Solution
satisfies the IC.
\end{claim}
\proof
\qed

\begin{claim} (time-like propagation for $x=x'=0$)
\label{cl-time-like-prop-wave}

\beq
\partial_{x'}G\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'} 
=
-\partial_x G\twoevent{0}{t}{0}{t'}=
 \delta(t-t')
\eeq 
As a consequence of this,
the General Solution satisfies the BC.
Furthermore, 
the Green's Function
must satisfy the homogeneous
version of the inhomogeneous BC
satisfied by $\eta(x,t)$.
\end{claim}
\proof
\qed
\end{mybluearea}

\subsection{Point sources and their images}

All Green's functions must satisfy
the DE with delta function source

\beq
\underbrace{(\partial_t-D\partial_x^2)}_{L_{x,t}} G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t')
\quad \text{for all $x>0$ and $t>0$}
\label{eq-LG-deltas}
\eeq
In addition, they must satisfy the homogeneous BC
that match the 
inhomogeneous BC satisfied by the
solution $\eta(x,t)$.

Let $\caln(x;\s^2)$
represent the Normal Distribution
with mean zero and standard deviation $\s$:
\beq
\caln(x;\s^2)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\s^2}}
\exp\left(\frac{-x^2}{2\s^2}\right)
\eeq


Let 
\beqa
\underbrace{G^+\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}}_{g_0(x-x', t-t')}
&=& \caln(x- x'; \kappa(t-t'))\indi(t>t')
\label{eq-def-green-pm}
\eeqa
where $\kappa =2D$.  
$G^+=G_0$ is called the {\bf free space (no BC) Green's function}. It 
will be used as a building block
to construct the Green's functions
appropriate to the various BC.

$G^+$ is centered at $x= x'$.
$G^-$, centered at $x'=-x$, is the image of $G^+$
with respect to the $x=0$ plane.

\beq
G^-\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=
G^+ \twoevent{-x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq



Note that the functions $G^\pm$
satisfy Eq.(\ref{eq-LG-deltas})
(with $x'\rarrow -x'$ for $G^-$) so they are bona fide Green's functions. Indeed,

\beq
L_{x,t}\underbrace{\caln(x\mp x'; \kappa(t-t'))}_{\caln_\mp}=0\quad \text{for $x>0$ 
and $t>0$}
\eeq
and

\beq
\caln_\mp\rarrow \delta(x\mp x') \text{ as $t\to t'$}
\eeq
Therefore, 

\beqa
L_{x,t}\left[\caln_\mp \indi(t-t'>0)\right]&=& \caln_\mp\partial_t\indi(t-t'>0)
\\
&=&
\caln_\mp \delta(t-t')
\\
&=& \delta(x\mp x')\delta(t-t')
\eeqa

\beq
G_D =G^+-G^-, \quad  G_N=G^++ G^-
\eeq
Note that the $G^-$ spike
occurs at $x=-x'<0$ so
\beq
L_{x,t}G^+= \delta(x-x')\delta(t-t'),\quad L_{x,t}G^-= 0, \quad \text{for all $x>0$ and $t>0$}
\eeq
Hence, both $G_D$ and $G_N$ satisfy
Eq.(\ref{eq-LG-deltas}) 
in the region of interest (i.e., $x>0$ and $t>0$).

\underbrace{(\partial_t^2-\partial_x^2)}_{L_{x,t}} G\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t')
\quad \text{for all $x>0$ and $t>0$}
\label{eq-LG-deltas-wave}
\eeq



\beq
\underbrace
{G^+\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}}
_{g_0(x-x', t-t')}
=\frac{1}{2}\indi(t-t'> |x-x'|)
\eeq
\beq
G^+\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=
G^+\twoevent{\tp}{\tm}{\tpp}{\tmp}
=\frac{1}{2}\indi(\tp>\tpp)\indi(\tm>\tmp)
\eeq

\beq
G^-\twoevent{ x}{t}{x'}{t'}=
G^+\twoevent{- x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\eeq


Note that the function $G^+$
satisfies Eq.(\ref{eq-LG-deltas-wave}) so it is a  bona fide Green's function. Indeed, using the

\underbrace{L_{x,t}}_{\partial_t^2-\partial_x^2}
G^+\twoevent{x-x'}{t}{0}{0}=

\eeq

\subsection{Boundary Conditions}
\subsubsection{Dirichlet BC}
For the Dirichlet BC, $\eta$
must satisfy
\beq
\eta(0, t)= D_\rvt(t)
\eeq
and the Green's function $G_D$ must satisfy
\beq
G_D\twoevent{0}{t}{x'}{t'}=0
\eeq
A possible solution is

\beq
G_D= G^+ - G^-
\eeq


This $G_D$ was found by the method of images: two
free space solutions,
$G^+$ and its image
$G^-$
with respect to the boundary $x=x'$,
were superposed so as  to satisfy
$G_D=0$ at the boundary $x=0$.




\begin{claim}
Prove that
\beq
D
\int_0^{t} dt'\;
\partial_{x'} G_D\twoevent{0}{t}{0}
{t'}
=\delta(t-t')
\eeq
using the explicit form of $G_D= G^+-G^-$.
\end{claim}
\proof

Suppose

\beq
G^+\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}= \caln(x-x'; \kappa (t-t'))
\eeq
where $D=\kappa/2$.
Define

\beq
y=\frac{x}{\sqrt{\kappa(t-t')}}=\frac{x}{\ell}
\eeq
Then
\beqa
D\left[\partial_{x'} G^+\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\right]_{x'=0}&=&
\left(\frac{\kappa}{2}\right)
\left(\frac{(-1)^2 x}{\ell^2}\right)\frac{1}{\ell\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\left(
\frac{-x^2}{2\ell^2}\right)
\\
&=&
\frac{x}{2(t-t')}\frac{1}{\ell\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\left(
\frac{-y^2}{2}\right)
\eeqa

\beq
\ln y = -\frac{1}{2}\ln (t-t')+\cdots\implies \frac{dy}{y}=
-\;\frac{d(t-t')}{2(t-t')}=
\;\frac{dt'}{2(t-t')}
\eeq
Thus,

\beq
dt'\;\frac{x}{2(t-t')}= dy\;\frac{x}{y}=
dy\sqrt{\kappa(t-t')}=dy\ell
\eeq
Hence
\beq
D\int_0^t dt'\left[\partial_{x'} G^+\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}
\right]_{x'=0}=
\int_{\frac{x}{\sqrt{\kappa t}}}^\infty
dy \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\left(
\frac{-y^2}{2}\right)=\frac{1}{2}
\eeq
\subsubsection{Neumann BC}
For the Neumann BC, $\eta$ must satisfy
\beq
\partial_x\eta(0, t)= N_\rvt(t)
\eeq
and the Green's function $G_N$ must satisfy
\beq
G_N\twoevent{x}{t}{0}{t'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}=0
\eeq
A possible solution is

\beq
G_N= G^+ + G^-
\eeq
Note that for the Dirichlet BC problem, $G^+$ and $G^+$
are subtracted 
whereas here they are added.

$LG=\delta$ and the Neumann BC
only constrain derivatives of $G$.
Hence, $G_N +c$ and
$G_N$ for some constant $c$, both 
satisfy those constraints; i.e., $G_N$  is 
defined only  up to a constant. This
ambiguity  in $G_N$
is referred to as a {\bf zero-mode}.
 


\subsubsection{Mixed (Robin) BC}
For the Robin BC, $\eta$ must satisfy
\beq
a_0\eta(0, t) + a_1\partial_x \eta(0, t)= R_\rvt(t)
\eeq
and the Green's function $G_R$ must satisfy
\beq
[a_0 +  a_1 \partial_x]G_R\twoevent{0}{t}{x'}{t'}=0
\eeq

Let 
\beq
G_0\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=
g_0(x-x', t-t)
\eeq
denote the free space Green's function
and let
\beq
G_\mu\twoevent{x}{t}{x'}{t'}=
g_\mu(x, x'; t-t) \quad\text{for $\mu = D, N, R$}
\eeq
denote the Dirichlet, Neumann 
and Robin Green's functions.

For $\tau=t-t'>0$,
define the following Laplace transforms
\beq
\underbrace{\call[g_0(x-x', \cdot)](s)}_
{\tilde{g}_0(x-x',s)}=\int_0^\infty d\tau\; e^{-s\tau}g_0(x-x', \tau)
\eeq

\beq
\underbrace{\call[g_\mu(x,x'; \cdot)](s)}_
{\tilde{g}_\mu(x, x';s)}=\int_0^\infty d\tau\; e^{-s\tau}g_\mu(x-x'; \tau)
\quad\text{for $\mu = D, N, R$}
\eeq
Let
\beq
K= \sqrt{\frac{s}{D}},\quad
L_{x,t}=\partial_t -\partial_x^2
\eeq





\beq
K_0= \frac{a_0}{a_1}
\eeq


\begin{claim}\label{cl-lp-robin-de}
If
$g_0$ satisfies

\beq
L_{x,t}g_0(x-x';\tau)=0
\eeq
and 
\beq
[a_0 + a_1\partial_x]g_0(x-x';\tau)=0,
\eeq
then

\beq
\tilde{g}_R(x, x';s)=
\tilde{g}_0(x-x', s)
+\tilde{R}(s)\tilde{g}_0(x+x', s)
\eeq
where

\beq
\tilde{g}_0(x-x', s)=
\frac{1}{2DK}
e^{-K|x-x'|}
\eeq
and


\beq
\tilde{R}(s)=
\frac{K+K_0}{K-K_0}
\eeq
Hence, in the time domain, 
\beq
G_R= g_0(x-x', \tau)
+
\underbrace{\int_0^{\tau}
d\tau'\; R(\tau')
g_0(x+x', \tau-\tau')}_{\text{time convolution}}
\eeq
$R(\tau)$ is called the {\bf boundary response kernel} or {\bf reflection coefficient}.
\end{claim}
\proof

\beq
(\partial_t - D\partial_x^2)g(x, x'; t)=\delta(x-x')\delta(t-t')
\eeq
Apply 
$\int_0^\infty dt\;e^{-st}$
to both sides and integrate the time derivative by parts


\beq
(K^2-\partial_x^2)
\tilde{g}(x, x';s)=
\frac{1}{D}
\delta(x-x')
\eeq
where $K=\sqrt{s/D}$

\beq
\tilde{g}'' - K^2\tilde{g}= 
-\;\frac{1}{D}
\delta(x-x')
\eeq
The most
general
solution to this ODE is
as follows, assuming that
the solution has a kink
at  $x=x'$, and is
finite for $x\rarrow \infty$
\beq
\tilde{g}(x, x'; s)
=\left\{
\begin{array}{ll}
Ae^{Kx}+ Be^{-Kx}
&\text{if $0<x<x'$}
\\
C e^{-Kx}
&\text{if $x>x'$}
\end{array}
\right.
\eeq
To solve for the 3 unknowns $A,B,C$, we need
3 linear equations.
Those 3 equations are
provided by the Robin BC at $x=0$,
and 2 continuity conditions 
at $x=x'$.

The Robin BC means that
\beq
K_0 \tilde{g}|_{x=0}+ \tilde{g}'|_{x=0}=0
\eeq
so
 \beq K_0(A+B) + K(A-B)=0
\eeq

\beq
(K+K_0)A=(K-K_0)B
\eeq

\beq
\boxed
{B=RA, \quad R=\frac{K+K_0}{K-K_0}}
\eeq
\hrule

Let $E=e^{Kx'}$, $\ol{E}=e^{-Kx'}$
The behavior 
of $\tilde{g}$ at $x=x'$
must be as follows, in
order
to produce a $\delta(x-x')$
when differentiated twice.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\tilde{g}$ must
have a kink but be 
continuous at $x=x'$

\beq
\tilde{g}|_{x=x'-\eps}=
\tilde{g}|_{x=x'+\eps}
\eeq

\beq
A e^{Kx'}+ B e^{-Kx'}=
Ce^{-Kx'}
\eeq

\beq
\boxed
{A(E +R\ol{E})-C\ol{E}=0}
\label{eq-boxed-cont-eq1}
\eeq

\item 
$\tilde{g}'$ must jump at $x=x'$

\beq
\int_{x'-\eps}^{x'+\eps}dx\;  [\tilde{g}''-K^2\tilde{g}]= -\;\frac{1}{D}
\eeq
The integral over $K^2 \tilde{g}$ tends to zero as $\eps\to 0$ so

\beq
\tilde{g}'|_{x=x'+\eps}-
\tilde{g}'|_{x=x'-\eps}=-\;\frac{1}{D}
\eeq


\beq
(-K)Ce^{-Kx'}-(K)Ae^{Kx'}-(-K)Be^{-Kx'}=-\;\frac{1}{D}
\eeq

\beq
\boxed{-C\ol{E} +A(-E+R\ol{E})=-\frac{1}{DK}}
\label{eq-boxed-cont-eq2}
\eeq
\end{enumerate}
\hrule Eqs.(\ref{eq-boxed-cont-eq1})
 and Eq.(\ref{eq-boxed-cont-eq2})
constitute an easily solved system of two equations in 2 unknowns $A,C$.
\beq
\underbrace{\begin{pmatrix}
E + R\ol{E}&-\ol{E}
\\
-E + R\ol{E}&-\ol{E}
\end{pmatrix}}_{\calm}
\begin{pmatrix}
A
\\
C
\end{pmatrix}=
\begin{pmatrix}
0
\\
-1/(DK)
\end{pmatrix}
\eeq

\beq
\det\calm=-(1+R\ol{E}^2) -1+R\ol{E}^2=-2
\eeq
\beqa
\begin{pmatrix}
A
\\
C
\end{pmatrix}&=&
\frac{1}{-2}
\begin{pmatrix}
-\ol{E}&\ol{E}
\\
E-R\ol{E}&E+R\ol{E}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
0
\\
-1/(DK)
\end{pmatrix}
\\
&=&
\frac{1}{2DK}
\begin{pmatrix}
\ol{E}
\\
E + R\ol{E}
\end{pmatrix}
\eeqa

\beq
A= \frac{\ol{E}}{2DK}, \quad 
B= RA=R\frac{\ol{E}}{2DK},
\quad
C=\frac{E}{2DK}+R\frac{\ol{E}}{2DK}
\eeq

For $0<x<x'$
\beq
\tilde{g}=Ae^{Kx}+Be^{-Kx}=
\frac{1}{2DK}\left[
e^{K(x-x')} +R e^{-K(x+x')}
\right]
\eeq
and for $x>x'$
\beq
\tilde{g}=Ce^{-Kx}=
\frac{1}{2DK}\left[ 
e^{-K(x-x')}+Re^{-K(x+x')}\right]
\eeq
Hence, for all $x$

\beq
\tilde{g}=
\frac{1}{2DK}\left[ 
e^{-K|x-x'|}+R e^{-K(x+x')}\right]
\eeq



\qed

We can check
that $\tilde{g}$
has the expected
behavior in the
$K_0$ equals $0$ and $infty$ limits: 
\begin{itemize}
\item Dirichlet BC, $K_0\rarrow \pm\infty$, $\tilde{R}(s)\to -1$ so $R(\tau)=-\delta(\tau)$

\beq G_R\rarrow 
g_0(x-x', \tau)
-
g_0(x+x', \tau -\tau')
=G_D
\eeq
\item
Neumann BC, $K_0=0$
$\tilde{R}(s)=1$ so 
$R(\tau)=\delta(\tau)$

\beq G_R\rarrow 
g_0(x-x', \tau)
+
g_0(x+x', \tau )
=G_N
\eeq
\end{itemize}


From 
a table of Laplace
transforms, we know that

\beq
\call^{-1}_s\left[
\frac{1}{\sqrt{s}}\exp\left(
-a\sqrt{s}\right)\right](t)=
\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi t}}\exp\left(\frac{-a^2}{4t}\right)
\eeq
Therefore, 
\beq
\underbrace{\call^{-1}_s[\tilde{g}_0(b,s)](\tau)}_{g_0(b,\tau)}
=
\call^{-1}_s\left[
\left.
\frac{1}{2DK}e^{-bK}
\right|_{K=\sqrt{s/D}}
\right](\tau)
=
\frac{1}{\sqrt{4\pi D\tau}}\exp\left(
\frac{-b^2}{4D\tau}
\right)
\eeq

Note that $K_0=a_0/a_1$ can be positive or negative
and that changing the
sign of $K_0$
inverts the refection
coefficient $K_0$
\beq
R(-K_0)=\frac{1}{R(K_0)}
\eeq

Note also that $R$
can be easily rewritten
as 1 plus something
\beq
R= \frac{K-K_0+2K_0}{K-K_0}=
1 +\frac{2K_0}{K-K_0}
\label{eq-r-2k0-div-sum}
\eeq

\beq
R= \frac{-(K-K_0)+2K}{K-K_0}=
-1 +\frac{2K}{K-K_0}
\eeq
By virtue of Eq.(\ref{eq-r-2k0-div-sum}), we can re-express $\tilde{g}_R$ as
\beq
\tilde{g}_R(x, x';s)=
\tilde{g}_N(x-x', s)
+\frac{2K_0}{K-K_0}\tilde{g}_0(x+x', s)
\label{eq-r-n-plus-boundary-term}
\eeq
According to
Eq.(\ref{eq-r-n-plus-boundary-term}), $\tilde{g}_R$
equals $\tilde{g}_N$  plus a boundary term that vanishes when $K_0=a_0=0$.
$\tilde{g}_N$  has 2 identical point sources
at $x'=\pm x$ interfering constructively.



\begin{mybluearea}

For the Wave Equation, one must make the following modifications.

\beq
K=\frac{s}{c}=s,\quad
L_{x,t}=\partial_t^2 -\partial_x^2
\eeq
From a table of Laplace transforms,
\beq
\call^{-1}_s\left[\frac{1}{s}\exp(-as)\right](t)=
\indi(t>a)
\eeq
so

\beq
\underbrace{\call^{-1}_s[\tilde{g}_0(a,s)](\tau)}_{g_0(a,\tau)}
=
\call^{-1}_s\left[\left.
\frac{1}{2K}e^{-Kb}
\right|_{K=s}
\right](\tau)
=
\frac{1}{2}\indi(\tau>b)
\eeq

For the Wave Equation, $K=s$,
so (unlike the
DE case where $K\propto \sqrt{s}$) we can invert the Laplace transform of $\tilde{R}$ in closed form. 
From a table of Laplace transforms,
\beq
\call_s^{-1}\left[\frac{1}{s-a}\right]=
e^{at}
\eeq
so

\beq
G_R= G_N
+
2K_0\int_0^{\tau}
d\tau'\; e^{K_0 \tau'}
g_0(x+x', \tau-\tau')
\eeq
As $K_0\to 0$,

\beq
G_R\to G_D
\eeq
As $K_0\to -\infty$,
we have
$-K_0 e^{K_0\tau}\to \delta(\tau)$. Hence, 

\beq
G_R \to G_N -2G^- = G_D
\eeq

\end{mybluearea}

\subsection{General Solution}


\begin{claim}
Suppose
\beq
L_{x}= -\partial_x^2,\quad
L_{x} G(x, x')=\delta(x-x')
\eeq
The {\bf General Solution} to the equation
\beq
L_{x} \eta (x) = S(x)\quad
\eeq
for $x>0$, satisfying
Dirichlet, Neumann or Robin boundary conditions at $x=0$, is:

\beqa
\ket{\eta}
&=&
\underbrace{G{P_\rvx}\ket{S}}_{\text{Source Term $\cala_S$}}
\\
&&+\underbrace{
G\left[-\ket{x'}\partial_{x'}\bra{x'}
+
\ket{x'}\cev{\partial}_{x'}\bra{x'}\right]_{x'=0}
\ket{\eta}}_{\text{Boundary Conditions (BC) term $\cala_{BC}$}}
\eeqa
or, in non-Dirac notation,

\beqa
\eta(x)
&=&
\int_0^\infty dx'\;G(x,x')S(x')
\\
&&+
G(x,0) \left[-\partial_{x'}
+ \cev{\partial}_{x'}
\right]\eta(0)
\eeqa
\end{claim}
\proof
\qed

\subsection{Boundary Conditions}
\subsubsection{Dirichlet BC}

For the Dirichlet BC, $\eta(x)$
must satisfy
\beq
\eta(0)= A
\eeq
and the Green's function $G_D(x)$ must satisfy
\beq
G_D(0)=0
\eeq
A possible solution is
\beqa
G_D(x, x')
&=&G^+ -G^-
\\
&=&\min(x, x')
\quad\text{for $x>0$ (see Eqs.(\ref{eq-four-distance-funs}))}
\eeqa

As can be seen from Fig.\ref{fig-min-max-x-xp}
and Fig.\ref{fig-distance-funs},
$G^+-G^-$ has slope $+1$  for $x'\in [-x, x]$
and is constant for $x'<-x$ and $x'>x$.
This kink at $x'=\pm x$
leads to a jump in the first derivative $\partial_{x'}G$ and to a delta in the 
second derivative $\partial_{x'}^2G$.
Hence $L G=\delta(x-x') + \delta(x+x')$.
Since we are working in the $x'>0$ quadrant,
the mirror image delta function $\delta(x+x')$ can be 
ignored.


\subsubsection{Neumann BC}
For the Neumann BC, $\eta(x)$
must satisfy
\beq
\partial_x\eta(0)= B
\eeq
and the Green's function $G_N(x)$ must satisfy
\beq
\partial_xG_N(0)=0
\eeq
A possible solution is

\beqa
G_N(x, x')&=&G^++G^- 
\\
&=&
-\max(x, x')
\quad\text{for $x>0$ (see Eqs.(\ref{eq-four-distance-funs}))}
\eeqa

As can be seen from Fig.\ref{fig-min-max-x-xp}
and Fig.\ref{fig-distance-funs},
$G^++G^-$ is constant  for $x'\in [-x, x]$
and has slope $\pm 1$ for $x'<-x$ and $x'>x$.
This kink at $x'=\pm x$
leads to a jump in the first derivative $\partial_{x'}G$ and to a delta in the 
second derivative $\partial_{x'}^2G$.
Hence $L G=\delta(x-x') + \delta(x+x')$.
Since we are working in the $x'>0$ quadrant,
the mirror image delta function $\delta(x+x')$ can be 
ignored.


\subsubsection{Mixed (Robin)}

\begin{claim}

\beq 
G_R=\frac {a_0 x'\min (x,x') - a_1 \max(x, x')}{a_0 x' + a_1}
\eeq
\end{claim}
\proof
Let 
\beq
\Omega_x= a_0 + a_1\partial_x,,
\quad L_x = -\partial_x^2
\eeq
We know that
$G_D$ satisfies the Robin BC
constraint at $x=0$
and the $Lg=\delta$ constraint 
at $x=x'$ for $x>0$. (this
can be verified visually
from Fig.\ref{fig-min-max-x-xp})

\beq
\Omega_x\min(0, x') =0, \quad L_x \min(x, x')=\delta(x-x')
\eeq
Likewise, we know that $G_N$
satisfies those 2 constraints too.

\beq
-\Omega_x\max(0, x') =0, \quad -L_x \max(x, x')=\delta(x-x')
\eeq
Assume\footnote{Note that
the constraint $\lim_{x\to \infty}G =0$  is NOT being used. Neither $G_D=\min(x, x')$
nor $G_N=-\max(x,x')$ satisfy it. Physically, $\lim_{x\to \infty}G =0$ must be true, but a modification of the PDE model is required to exhibit it.}

\beq
G_R = A \min(x, x') - B\max(x,x')  
\eeq
From Fig.\ref{fig-min-max-x-xp},
it is easy to verify that

\beq
\Omega G_R|_{x=0}=0\implies\quad a_1 A
-a_0x'B=0
\eeq
and
\beq
L_xG_R|_{x=x'}=\delta(x-x')
\implies\quad A+B =1
\eeq
Hence,

\beq
A= \frac{a_0 x'}{a_0 x'+ a_1},\quad
B= \frac{a_1}{a_0 x' +a_1}
\eeq
\qed

Free space Geeen's function

\beq
\underbrace{-\sum_{i=1}^d \partial^2_{x_i}G}_{-\nabla^2G(\vecr, \vecrp)}=
\underbrace{\prod_{1=1}^d\delta(x_i-x_i')}_{\delta^d(\vecr, \vecrp)}
\eeq

$\engdim{G}=L^{2-d}$

\begin{itemize}
\item 1-dim
\beq
G^+(x, x')= -\;\frac{1}{2}|x-x'| = g_0(x-x')
\eeq
\item 2-dim
\beq
G^+(\vecr,\vecrp)= -\;\frac{1}{2\pi}\ln |\vecr
-\vecrp| = g_0(\vecr-\vecrp)
\eeq
\item 3-dim
\beq
G^+(\vecr,\vecrp)= \;\frac{1}{4\pi |\vecr
-\vecrp|} = g_0(\vecr-\vecrp)
\eeq


\item $d$-dim
\beq
G(R)=
\frac{1}{(d-2)S_d}
\frac{1}{R^{d-2}}
\eeq

\beq S_d= \frac{2\pi^{d/2}}
{\Gamma(d/2)}
\eeq

Numerous systems in Nature
behave as if they lived
in a non-integral dimension.
This usually happens
when the system zig-zags a lot
in going from point $A$ to point $B$. Such fractal
trajectories occur in Brownian motion,
percolation, turbulence,
near any critical point, etc.
\end{itemize}

Fig.\ref{fig-green-fun-123dim}
and Fig.\ref{fig-greens-fun-laplacian-continuous-dim}
show the free space Green's functions $G$ for minus Laplacian as a function of $R=|\vecr-\vecrp|$
in various dimensions, including fractional ones.
